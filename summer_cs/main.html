Syllabus

Overview

We will following along with material from Berkeley's introductory computer science course, CS 61A. This is a 4 unit class at Berkeley, meaning that college students should spend, on average, about 12 hours per week on this course. This is a difficult, college-level course . You can probably expect to spend more time than that. From this, you can expect to gain the following (in order of importance):

  1. Learn the format and rigors of college-style courses,
  2. Learn the basics of computer science and good programming practices,
  3. Learn how to program in Python, SQL, and Scheme

Course Format

Lecture: There are three 50-minute lecture videos per week. The links to these videos will be posted weekly. Please watch these videos on or before the day they are assigned.
Section: There a live lab and discussion session per week, tentatively on Tuesday and Thursday evenings.
Office Hours: In office hours, you can ask questions about the material and receive guidance on assignments one-on-one. Email me for more details.

Assignments

Labs: Lab exercises are designed to introduce a new topic. You can complete and submit these during the scheduled lab sections. These are scored on completion.
Labs: Homeworks are weekly assignments meant to help you apply the concepts learned in lecture and section on more challenging problems. These will be difficult; if you are stuck on a problem, come get help instead of copying the answer from someone else or the internet. These are scored on completion.
Projects: Projects are larger assignments intended to combine ideas from the course in interesting ways. Projects are graded on both correctness and composition.
Exams: There will be two midterms and a final exam.
Participation: A small fraction of your final grade will be determined by participation in lab and discussion.
